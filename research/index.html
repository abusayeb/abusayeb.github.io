Research interests

I work toward intelligent autonomous systems that perceive, reason about and act in the physical world. My interests span robot perception and embodied AI, computer vision for unstructured real-world settings — multi-object tracking, human activity recognition and pose estimation — and multimodal vision-language models. I am particularly drawn to generative models for simulation and robotic learning, and to the question of how synthetic data can close the data gap that constrains embodied agents.

This direction grew out of two complementary experiences: an undergraduate thesis fine-tuning diffusion models for controllable image synthesis, and a year building perception pipelines that had to survive contact with real deployment — noisy video, occlusion, and latency budgets. That combination is what draws me to embodied AI, where perception, generation and action have to work together rather than in isolation.

Robot Perception
Multi-object tracking, pose estimation and activity recognition for agents operating in cluttered, unstructured environments.
Embodied AI
Linking perception to action; grounding agent behaviour in continuous sensory streams rather than curated benchmarks.
